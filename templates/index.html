About the Prediction:

This prediction is generated using a Random Forest machine learning model trained on 517 historical forest fire incidents. The model analyzes eight critical environmental factors from the Fire Weather Index (FWI) system including fuel moisture codes (FFMC, DMC, DC), fire behavior indices (ISI), and meteorological conditions (temperature, humidity, wind, rainfall).

Our system achieves 93% accuracy with a ROC-AUC score of 0.717, demonstrating reliable classification performance. The model considers complex interactions between weather conditions and fuel moisture to estimate fire probability. Higher FFMC values, low humidity, strong winds, and elevated temperatures typically increase fire risk, while recent rainfall reduces it.

This tool is designed for environmental monitoring and early warning systems to help forest management teams make informed decisions about fire prevention measures and resource allocation.
